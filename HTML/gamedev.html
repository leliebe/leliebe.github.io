Sample Documents

Baldur's Gate II

Baldur's Gate II (2000)

Developer: BioWare

Publisher: Black Isle Studios, Interplay Entertainment

Genre: RPG

Platform: Windows, Mac

Design Documents: More of a postmortem than actual design documents, much of the discussion here reflects what was important in the design process.

The Colonel's Bequest

The Colonel's Bequest (1989)

Developer: Sierra On-Line

Publisher: Sierra On-Line

Genre: Point-and-click Adventure

Platform: DOS, Amiga, Atari ST

Design Docments: The design documents for The Colonel's Bequest are heavily annotated and include a novel-like script and handdrawn maps and storyboards.

Crankin's Time Travel Adventure

Crankin's Time Travel Adventure (2022)

Developer: Uvula

Genre: Experimental

Platform: Playdate

Design Documents: Keita Takahashi's design documents for this experimental game are quite comprehensive and reflect both the experimental nature of the platform as well as the small development team.

Destiny

Destiny (2014)

Developer: Bungie

Publisher: Activision

Genre: First-person shooter

Platform: PS3, PS4, Xbox360, Xbox One

Design Documents: David Candland has released some UX/UI design documentation for his work on Destiny.

Deus Ex

Deus Ex (2000)

Developer: Ion Storm

Publisher: Eidos Interactive

Genre: Action RPG

Platform: Windows, Mac, PS2

Design Documents: These are early design documents for Majestic Revelations, the game that would become Deus Ex. They are heavily annotated and focus on the initial concept, rather than detailed systems construction.

Diablo

Diablo (1997)

Developer: Blizzard

Publisher: Blizzard

Genre: RPG

Platform: Windows, Playstation, Mac OS

Design Documents: Diablo's initial design documents focus on establishing tone, marketing points, and the primary game-play-loop. It also includes a development schedule.

Doom

Doom (1993)

Developer: id Software

Publisher: id Software

Genre: First-person Shooter

Platform: "But does it run Doom?" (Originally for MS-DOS)

Design Documents: The "Doom Bible" is an early design document for Doom, created by Tom Hall. Notably, it is quite different from the final game, including multiple player characters, a relatively complex story, and a number of objects and enemies that were never implemented.

Dwarf Fortress

Dwarf Fortress (Ongoing)

Developer: Bay 12 Games

Publisher: Bay 12 Games

Genre: Simulation

Platform: Windows, Linux

Design Documents: The Dwarf Fortress Development blog isn't quite a traditional design document, but it shares some qualities with them. In some ways, this may be a more practical design document for a game still in active development, with progress marked by different colors and the connections between different development tasks clearly flagged.

Grand Theft Auto

Grand Theft Auto [Working Title: Race'n'Chase] (1997)

Developer: DMA Design

Publisher: BMG Interactive, ASC Games, Take-Two Interactive, Rockstar Games

Platform: MS-DOS, Playstation, Game Boy Color

Design Documents: The design documents for Race'n'Chase were originally released by Mike Dailly on Flickr, but his site has since been taken down. Several copies of the document exist elsewhere, including at the linked blog.

Grim Fandango

Grim Fandango (1998)

Developer: LucasArts

Publisher: LucasArts

Genre: Puzzle Adventure

Platform: Windows, Linux, OS X, PS4, PS Vita, Android, iOS, Nintendo Switch, Xbox One

Design Documents: Grim Fandango's design documents are very artistic in their presentation and focus primarily on the puzzle structure and story development.

Halo Series

Halo 2 (2004) and Halo 3 (2007)

Developer: Bungie

Publisher: Microsoft Game Studios

Genre: First-person shooter

Platform: Xbox, Windows

Design Documents: David Candland has uploaded a few design documents for his work on UX/UI design for the Halo series.

King's Quest IV

King's Quest IV (1988)

Developer: Sierra On-Line

Publisher: Sierra On-Line

Genre: Point-and-click Adventure

Platform: DOS, Amiga, Apple II, Atari ST

Design Documents: These partial design documents appear to have been curated well after development and serve as a reflection on the game's design process. However, they also include discussion of the coding practices used by the Sierra Creative Interpreter, the engine for many of Sierra's adventure games.

King's Quest VI

King's Quest VI (1992)

Developer: Sierra On-Line

Publisher: Sierra On-Line

Genre: Point-and-click Adventure

Platform: DOS, Amiga

Design Documents: This partial design document provides an overview of the plot and gameplay conventions for King's Quest VI, as well as narrative story descriptions and point-by-point descriptions of the puzzles in the game's first act.

Planescape: Torment

Planescape: Torment [Working Title: Last Rites] (1999)

Developer: Black Isle Studios

Publisher: Interplay Entertainment

Genre: RPG

Platform: Windows, Android, iOS, Linux, MacOS, Nintendo Switch, PS4, Xbox One

Design Documents: The design documents for Planescape: Torment are heavily focused on establishing the tone of the Planescape universe through tongue-in-cheek commentary. Discussion of technical elements is cursory.

Portal

Portal [Initial Title: Narbacular Drop] (2004)

Developer: Nuclear Monkey Software

Publisher: Nuclear Monkey Software

Genre: Puzzle

Platform: Windows

Design Documents: A wealth of documentation for Narbacular Drop has been made available by the original development team. Because this game was initially a student design project, it may be of particular interest to beginning game developers.

Prince of Persia

Prince of Persia (1989)

Developer: Broderbund

Publisher: Broderbund

Genre: Platformer

Platform: DOS, Apple II (original). Now ported to virtually all platforms.

Design Documents: Jordan Mechner's site includes sample design documents for both Prince of Persia and Karateka, as well as a link to the Apple II source code and technical documentation for Prince of Persia.

Rogue Legacy

Rogue Legacy (2013)

Developer: Cellar Door Games

Publisher: Cellar Door Games

Genre: Roguelike Platformer

Platform: Windows, Linux, OS X, Playstation 3, Playstation 4, Playstation Vita, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch, iOS

Design Documents: Curated and annotated design notes from Teddy Lee, the designer of Rogue Legacy. Serves as a humorous reflection on the game's development process.

Torin's Passage

Torin's Passage (1995)

Developer: Sierra On-Line

Publisher: Sierra On-Line

Genre: Point-and-click Adventure

Platform: MS-DOS, Windows 3.x, Windows 95, Mac

Design Documents: This narrative-focused design document also includes explanations of the game interface, all of the game objects, and detailed gameplay maps. Additionally, it includes a cinematic description of each cutscene and game trailer, complete with timing and camera angles.

What Remains of Edith Finch

What Remains of Edith Finch (2017)

Developer: Giant Sparrow

Publisher: Annapurna Interactive

Genre: Exploration

Platform: Windows, PS4, PS5, Xbox One, Nintendo Switch, iOS

Design Documents: This early concept document focuses on establishing the tone of the game and the key challenges it will face in terms of design, technology, and art.
